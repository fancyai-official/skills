# Kidswear Commercial Editorial Standards Guide

> Based on core understandings from user LV successful cases
> 
> **Keywords: Designed Naturalness / Choreographed Relaxation / Commercial but not Rigid**

---

## Core Philosophy

### ❌ Common Misconceptions

| Misconception Type | Erroneous Understanding | Consequence |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Misconception 1** | Commercial editorial = Rigid and stiff | Model looks like a wooden figure, lifeless |
| **Misconception 2** | Commercial editorial = Cold and restrained | Child loses innocence, overly adultified |
| **Misconception 3** | Commercial editorial = Completely static | Image lacks vitality and storytelling |
| **Misconception 4** | Natural = Daily snapshot | Becomes a family album-style casual shot |

### ✅ Correct Understanding

```
Commercial editorial = Choreographed "seemingly natural"

- Actions: Can be dynamic, but must capture the "most beautiful moment frozen"
- Expressions: Can smile, but must have a "designed naturalness"
- Emotions: Warm and healing, but not an everyday lifestyle look
- Overall: Every element is designed, yet presents a relaxed and natural feel
```

---

## Commercial Editorial vs. Daily Snapshot Comparison

### 1. Poses and Actions

#### ❌ Daily Snapshot Style
```
- Actually running and jumping (blurry motion process)
- Actually playing (random movements)
- Actually using props (functional interaction)
- Snapshot moment (uncontrollable randomness)
```

#### ✅ Commercial Editorial Style
```
- Dynamic frozen highlights (peak of a jump, middle frame of a spin)
- Choreographed poses (every joint angle is considered)
- Props as atmospheric elements (not actual usage)
- Most beautiful moment presentation (repeatable classic poses)
```

**Example Comparison:**

| Scene | ❌ Daily Version | ✅ Commercial Version |
|------|---------|---------|
| Sportswear | Actually playing basketball and shooting (fast movement) | Static power pose preparing to shoot holding the ball |
| Princess dress | Running around in the garden | Elegant frozen frame of a spinning skirt |
| Dynamic pose | Randomly jumping or exaggerated actions | Dynamic power pose with both feet grounded, body leaning forward to show athletic tension (NOT jumping off the ground) |

---

### 2. Expressions and Emotions

#### ❌ Daily Snapshot Style
```
- Laughing out loud, grinning widely
- Funny faces, goofy expressions
- Looking around, unfocused
- Overly excited, out of control state
```

#### ✅ Commercial Editorial Style (Kidswear Adapted)
```
- Gentle smile (smile in the eyes, corners of mouth naturally raised)
- Healing smile (warm but not exaggerated)
- Bright and focused gaze (focused, has storytelling)
- Relaxed and natural but with a sense of control
```

**Expression Design Standards:**

| Type | Specific Description | Keywords |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Smiling** | Mouth corners raised slightly (no teeth or slightly showing teeth), smile in the eyes, eyebrows and eyes relaxed | Gentle, healing, friendly |
| **Focused** | Eyebrows naturally stretched, eyes bright and sharp, lips naturally relaxed or slightly pursed | Serious, engaged, storytelling |
| **Relaxed** | Eyebrows and eyes relaxed, mouth corners straight or slightly raised, eyes soft | Confident, composed, high-end feel |
| **Curious** | Eyebrows slightly raised, eyes bright and energetic, mouth corners naturally relaxed | Agile, exploring, childlike innocence |

**❗Important:** Smiling and bright expressions are allowed, but avoid:
- Overly exaggerated expressions (mouth wide open, eyes closed laughing)
- Out of control emotions (overly excited, screaming state)
- Unfocused eyes (wandering gaze, looking elsewhere)

---

### 3. Eyes and Camera Awareness

#### ❌ Daily Snapshot Style
```
- Ignoring the camera's presence
- Wandering gaze
- Looking at parents/friends
- Looking around randomly
```

#### ✅ Commercial Editorial Style
```
- Strong camera awareness
- Eyes have a clear direction:
  1. Looking directly at the camera (confident, communicative)
  2. Looking at a point off-camera (storytelling, imaginative space)
  3. Looking at props/environment (interactive but designed)
- Eyes are bright and energetic, clearly focused
```

---

### 4. Scene Interaction

#### ❌ Daily Snapshot Style
```
- Actually "playing" with props
- Functionally using items
- Random environmental interaction
```

#### ✅ Commercial Editorial Style
```
- Props as "atmospheric elements"
- Designed interaction poses
- Environment is a "background story", not an object of use
```

**Examples:**

| Prop | ❌ Daily Version | ✅ Commercial Version |
|------|---------|---------|
| Basketball | Actually playing and dribbling | Holding the ball in a static pose, ball surface facing the camera to show the logo |
| Flower | Actually smelling or picking flowers | Hand gently touching petals, forming a visual element |
| Car | Actually "driving" | Posing inside the car, car is an atmospheric prop |
| Book | Actually reading | Holding a book, book is an artistic atmospheric element |

## Core Insights from User LV's Prompts

### LV Prompt Feature Analysis

#### **Set 1 (In-Car Scene):**
```
✅ Key Descriptions:
"Sitting in the driver's seat" (position setting)
"Body slightly leaning forward, shoulders slightly tense" (precise posture control)
"Arm reaching towards the steering wheel" (hand action design)
"Head turned slightly to the right" (head angle design)
"Calm, slightly tense" (emotional keywords)
"Eyes looking at a target outside the car" (gaze direction)

→ Not "actually driving"
→ But "designed static posture while sitting in the driver's seat"
```

#### **Set 2 (Standing by the Car):**
```
✅ Key Descriptions:
"Back lightly leaning against the car door edge" (leaning position)
"Legs crossed stance" (classic fashion pose)
"One hand in pocket" (hand posture)
"Casual high-end feel" (emotional positioning)
"Eyes looking directly at the camera" (strong camera awareness)

→ Not "casually standing by the car looking around"
→ But "carefully designed classic fashion stance"
```

#### **Set 3 (Courtyard Seat):**
```
✅ Key Descriptions:
"Half-lying/sitting on a white metal folding chair" (sitting posture design)
"Body slightly leaning back" (body angle)
"Left arm naturally hanging down and resting on the side of the chair" (left hand position)
"Right arm holding a puppy" (right hand action + prop)
"Slightly tired yet relaxed high-end feel" (emotional atmosphere)
"Lazy and exquisite, intimate but not overly sweet" (overall tone)

→ Not "actually playing with a dog"
→ But "designed lazy and exquisite half-lying posture"
```

### Core Insight: Precision of Description

```
Success Formula = Precise Position + Precise Angle + Precise Emotion + Precise Atmosphere

Instead of saying "sitting"
Say "half-lying/sitting, body leaning back, left arm on the chair side, right arm holding a dog"

Instead of saying "standing"
Say "back lightly leaning against the car door, legs crossed, one hand in pocket"

Instead of saying "looking at the camera"
Say "eyes looking directly at the camera, calm with a hint of alertness"
```

## Correct Templates for Kidswear Commercial Editorials

### Template 1: Static Stance Series (Suitable for: Formal, Casual, Outerwear)

```
Action Description Template:
"[Stance type], feet [positional relationship], weight [distribution],
 [left hand action], [right hand action],
 shoulders [state], posture [overall feeling];
 head [angle], eyes [direction],
 expression [emotion type]"

Example:
"Standing naturally, feet slightly apart at shoulder width, weight slightly shifted to the right leg showing a relaxed feel,
 left hand naturally placed in trouser pocket (thumb exposed), right hand naturally hanging down or lightly touching the collar,
 shoulders relaxed, posture upright but not rigid;
 head facing the camera or slightly turned 15 degrees to the left, eyes looking directly at the camera or at a point off-screen,
 expression gently smiling, mouth corners naturally raised, eyes bright and energetic"
```

### Template 2: Dynamic Frozen Series (Suitable for: Sportswear, Vibrant Scenes)

```
Action Description Template:
"Capturing the [frozen moment] of [action type]:
 [body posture], [leg state], [arm position],
 [head action], [hair/garment dynamics],
 expression [emotion description], eyes [direction],
 overall demonstrating [power/vitality/fluidity] aesthetics"

Example:
"Capturing the frozen moment of preparation for a basketball shot:
 feet naturally apart, knees slightly bent in a power-gathering state (feet not leaving the ground),
 both hands holding the ball in front of the chest, arms slightly tense showing muscle lines but not rigid,
 upper body slightly leaning forward showing the fluid lines of athletic posture;
 head raised looking towards the basket, eyebrows slightly furrowed showing focus,
 expression seriously engaged, mouth slightly pursed or with a confident smile, eyes bright and sharp"
```

### Template 3: Elegant Posture Series (Suitable for: Dresses, Princess Style)

```
Action Description Template:
"Frozen moment of an [elegant action]:
 [body posture], [leg position] forming an elegant curve,
 [hand action description],
 head [angle], eyes [direction],
 expression [gentle/dreamy/elegant],
 overall demonstrating [childlike innocence/elegance/healing] feel"

Example:
"Capturing the elegant moment of spinning a skirt hem:
 standing sideways, left leg slightly forward forming an elegant S-curve,
 both hands lightly lifting the sides of the skirt (action natural without force, fingertips lightly touching the fabric),
 or one hand naturally hanging down, the other lightly touching the hair;
 head slightly tilted up 15 degrees, eyes looking at the camera or slightly upwards with a dreamy feel,
 expression gentle healing smile, smile in the eyes, mouth corners naturally raised"
```

### Template 4: Relaxed and Natural Series (Suitable for: Daily wear, Casual Style)

```
Action Description Template:
"[Relaxed action], showing a relaxed and natural feel:
 [body posture], [support/leaning description],
 [hand action],
 head [angle], eyes [direction],
 expression [relaxed/comfortable/warm],
 overall atmosphere [relaxed but aesthetically pleasing]"

Example:
"Relaxed leaning posture, showing a comfortable relaxed feel:
 body leaning sideways against a wall/tree trunk/railing, one leg slightly bent,
 one hand naturally in pocket or resting on the support, the other hand naturally hanging down,
 head slightly turned aside, eyes gently looking at the camera or into the distance,
 expression relaxed and comfortable, mouth corners naturally relaxed or slightly raised,
 overall demonstrating an 'effortless natural beauty'"
```

## Summary of Key Principles

### ✅ Must Do:

1. **Precisely describe every detail**
   - The position and angle of every body part
   - Specific states of fingers, line of sight, and center of gravity
   - Precise emotional keywords (3-5)

2. **Design a "seemingly natural" posture**
   - Can be dynamic, but must be the most beautiful frozen moment
   - Can smile, but must have a sense of design
   - Can be relaxed, but must have control

3. **Emphasize camera awareness**
   - Eyes must have a clear direction
   - Expression must be presented to the camera
   - Overall posture facing the camera

4. **Maintain kidswear characteristics**
   - Do not overly adultify (avoid "sexy", "seductive")
   - Maintain childlike innocence (warm, healing, vibrant)
   - Comply with a child's natural state (do not be overly rigid)

### ❌ Must Avoid:

1. **Daily snapshot descriptions**
   - "Playing happily"
   - "Running and jumping randomly"
   - "Laughing naturally"

2. **Overly rigid descriptions**
   - "Military standing posture"
   - "Expressionless"
   - "Completely still"

3. **Vague and broad descriptions**
   - "Natural stance"
   - "Looking at the camera"
   - "Smiling expression"

4. **Over-adultification**
   - "Sexy posture"
   - "Seductive eyes"
   - "Overly mature expression"

## Practical Checklist

When writing kidswear commercial editorial prompts, check the following items:

### Poses and Actions ✓
- [ ] Is there a description of a specific body posture (not just "standing" but "how to stand")
- [ ] Is the specific position of the hands explained (where are the left and right hands respectively)
- [ ] Is the weight distribution explained (legs even/leaning to one side)
- [ ] Is the head angle explained (facing front/turned aside by how many degrees)
- [ ] If dynamic, is "frozen moment" emphasized

### Expressions and Emotions ✓
- [ ] Are there specific emotional keywords (not "happy" but "gentle smile/focused and serious")
- [ ] Is the direction of the eyes described (looking directly at the camera/looking at a point)
- [ ] Are everyday descriptions like "laughing out loud", "goofy" avoided
- [ ] Are kidswear characteristics maintained (warm/healing/vibrant)

### Camera Awareness ✓
- [ ] Is the focus of the eyes emphasized
- [ ] Is a posture facing the camera explained
- [ ] Is there a "sense of storytelling" or "sense of communication"

### Prop Interaction ✓
- [ ] Are props used as "atmospheric elements" rather than for functional use
- [ ] Is a "designed interaction" with the prop described
- [ ] Are descriptions of "actually using" avoided

### Overall Atmosphere ✓
- [ ] Is there a clear "narrative theme"
- [ ] Is there a "sense of storytelling" rather than "snapshot"
- [ ] Is "designed naturalness" achieved

> **Seemingly natural, but actually meticulously designed**  
> **Warm and healing, but not casually everyday**  
> **Vibrant and dynamic, but not out-of-control snapshot**  
> **Complies with kidswear characteristics, displaying commercial texture**
