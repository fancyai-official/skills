# Pose vocabulary extension

For **internal** phrase breakdown into English prompts in `editorial-fashion-six`; must align with **brand, triple-choice, and shot framing**; **the set may rotate poses** (stand / sit / lean / walk, etc.) so not all six are the same stance with only crop changes. Still follow main Skill: **eyes mostly to camera**, full limbs, **no bag by default**, hat/hands do not cover chest logo and key structure. **Outfit visibility** must match [reference-outfit.md](reference-outfit.md) **§7**: chosen **pants/skirt/shoes/socks** stay visible and consistent in full body, seated, lean shots—no “teleporting” outfit.

---

## Usage principles

- **Unified mood, varied poses**: under one triple-choice scene, each frame may use a different **stance** (standing weight, seated, lean, slow walk)—short phrases only, don’t stack.
- **Match framing**: full body shows full shoes/pants; three-quarter/chest-up avoid “wide stride walk” that crops badly.
- **Eyes**: front/side/three-quarter/full body still favor `looking at camera, eye contact`; side shots use `over-shoulder glance`, `head turn toward camera`. **Pure back** shots only if client needs and assets support—see main Skill.
- **Safety**: prefer natural, repeatable anatomy; avoid extreme twist, kneel/crawl poses that often lose limbs.

## 6. Shot scale and pose pairing

| Scale | Suitable pose directions |
|-------|--------------------------|
| Chest-up / shoulders up | Slight turn, adjust collar, hand near collarbone; avoid big walk |
| American three-quarter / cowboy | Stand or bar stool, waist/hip clear; hands in pockets or on waist |
| Knee-up / full body | Stand, walk, bench sit, lean rail; shoes/pants must read when required |
| Fabric macro | Local chest/shoulder; if face in frame, still `looking at camera` |

## 7. E-commerce stable vs editorial relaxed

- **E-com, information-first**: stable stance weight, clear front and side, less big motion; seated neat or shallow.
- **Editorial**: mix walk, lean, seated; still **same shoes/socks/bottoms**—no random swaps.

## 14. Six-frame rotation example (varied feel)

Structural example only—adjust for whether client has back assets and main Skill shot table.

1. Front chest/waist: weight shift, pocket or collar hand.  
2. Side: three-quarter stand, over-shoulder to camera.  
3. Full body: walk freeze or step-in-place, shoes clear.  
4. Front variation or alt angle: rail lean look back / three-quarter back (**without back assets don’t force pure back**—use main Skill fallback).  
5. Three-quarter outfit: bar stool or stand hands on waist, waist/pockets visible.  
6. Fabric macro: still slight turn, chest/shoulder close.

---

## 15. Taboos and avoidance (match main Skill)

- Don’t pose over **chest logo, hero print, waistband**; hat brim and hands same rule.
- Avoid elbow/knee exactly on frame edge—“amputation” feel.
- **Shoes on feet only**; no floating shoes or holding shoes unless client asks.
- Children, client Excel extra pose bans—**follow sheet**.
