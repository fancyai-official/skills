## Fabric Families — Full Reference

| Family | Key Variants | Sheen | Drape | How It Photographs |
|--------|-------------|-------|-------|-------------------|
| **Silk** | Charmeuse (liquid sheen), Crepe de chine (matte fluid), Georgette (sheer matte), Chiffon (sheer float), Organza (stiff sheer), Taffeta (crisp sheen), Dupioni (slubbed sheen) | Charmeuse=high, Crepe=low, Georgette=none, Taffeta=medium-crisp | Charmeuse=pools, Crepe=falls, Georgette=floats, Organza=holds, Taffeta=sculpts | High-sheen silks reflect light sources — watch for blown highlights. Matte silks absorb light evenly. Sheer silks need careful lighting to control transparency. |
| **Cotton** | Poplin (smooth crisp), Twill (diagonal weave), Oxford (basket weave), Chambray (soft denim-look), Jersey (knit stretch), Canvas (heavy stiff), Denim (twill, indigo-dyed) | All matte except polished poplin | Poplin=crisp, Jersey=clings, Canvas=rigid, Denim=stiff-to-soft by wash | Cotton absorbs light — no sheen management needed. Texture shows best under raking/side light. Denim indigo shifts blue-purple on warm film stocks. |
| **Wool** | Worsted (smooth suit fabric), Flannel (soft napped), Bouclé (looped texture), Melton (dense felted), Cashmere (fine soft), Tweed (rough multi-color) | Worsted=subtle, others matte | Worsted=drapes, Flannel=falls soft, Bouclé=holds, Melton=rigid | Wool textures need side light to read. Bouclé/tweed become hero in close-up. Cashmere reads as "expensive air." |
| **Leather/Suede** | Full-grain (natural surface), Nappa (butter-soft), Patent (mirror gloss), Suede (napped matte), Vegan (coated fabric) | Patent=mirror, Full-grain=medium, Nappa=subtle, Suede=zero | Patent=stiff, Nappa=drapes, Suede=soft structure | Patent reflects EVERYTHING. Full-grain develops photogenic patina. Suede absorbs all light, needs strong directional light. |
| **Synthetics** | Neoprene (bonded foam), Scuba (stretch bonded), Tech jersey (performance knit), Nylon (crisp light), Polyester satin (synthetic sheen) | Neoprene=matte, Nylon=subtle, Poly-satin=artificial sheen | Neoprene=holds shape, Nylon=crisp, Tech jersey=clings | Synthetics look cheap if lit flat. Neoprene needs architectural lighting. Poly-satin sheen reads differently from real silk — cooler, more uniform. |
| **Knits** | Fine gauge (smooth), Cable (heavy textured), Rib (vertical stretch), Intarsia (color pattern) | All matte | Fine=drapes, Cable=holds, Rib=clings | Knit texture invisible in flat light — always use directional/raking light. Cable knit is inherently photogenic. |

## Lighting Setups — Full Reference

| Setup | What It Is | Best For Fabric Type | Mood |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|------|
| **Rembrandt** | 45° key light, triangle on shadow cheek | Textured fabrics (tweed, cable knit, canvas) — raking angle reveals weave | Classic, painterly, dramatic |
| **Butterfly/Paramount** | Key directly above and in front | Smooth fabrics (silk, satin, fine wool) — even light for sheen control | Glamorous, polished, beauty |
| **Clamshell** | Two soft sources above and below face | Delicate fabrics (chiffon, georgette, lace) — wraps without harsh shadows | Soft, luminous, editorial beauty |
| **Split** | Key at 90° to subject, half face lit | Structured garments (tailoring, leather) — shadow reveals 3D form | Moody, confrontational |
| **Beauty dish** | Large parabolic reflector, hard-soft hybrid | High-sheen fabrics (charmeuse, patent) — controlled specular highlight | Fashion-forward, clean but punchy |
| **Ring light** | Circular light around lens, shadow-free | Flat fabrics needing detail (print, embroidery) — shows every detail | Direct, confrontational, editorial |
| **Strip boxes** | Narrow softboxes on each side | Bodywear, knitwear, form-fitting — edge light defines silhouette | Sculpted, body-conscious |
| **Practicals only** | Existing light (lamps, neon, candles, windows) | Matte fabrics (cotton, wool, suede) — environment lights the garment | Documentary, atmospheric |
| **Direct flash** | On-camera flash, flat | Any fabric — deliberately flattens for anti-fashion effect | Raw, snapshot, Treatment B |
| **Natural window** | Single window source, unmodified | Fluid fabrics (silk, jersey) — shows drape and movement | Intimate, domestic, honest |
| **Cross-lighting** | Two opposing sources at equal intensity | Heavy/sculptural garments — eliminates flat spots, reveals full 3D form | Dramatic, architectural |
| **Negative fill** | Black card opposite key, deepens shadows | Matte/dark garments (navy, black) — prevents formlessness | Moody, shadow-rich |
