# Lighting Techniques

Detailed guide to lighting in commercial beverage photography, based on the practical experience of top photographers.

---

## Lighting Fundamentals

### Three Core Lighting Principles

**1. Transparency Principle**
Glass and liquid need transmitted or backlighting to reveal translucency — flat lighting makes them look 2D.

**2. Reflection Principle**
Use reflections from bottles and glassware to add layers, but control unwanted reflections.

**3. Layering Principle**
Build a three-dimensional feel through multiple light layers (key light, fill light, background light, rim light).

## Core Lighting Techniques

### Backlighting

**When to Use**
- Liquid splash photography
- Transparent liquid shooting
- Highlighting glassware
- Showcasing ice texture

**Key Points**
- Light source placed behind subject
- Creates glowing edge outlines
- Liquid appears crystalline and translucent
- Control flare — don't overdo it

**Setup**
```
Key light: behind product at 45°
Intensity: medium-high (avoid overexposure)
Diffusion: use soft panel or frosted material
Fill light: faint front fill to lift shadows
```

**Prompt Keywords**
- backlit, rim lighting, glowing edges
- translucent, luminous, light passing through
- silhouette with bright edges

**Best Products**
- Red wine liquid splash (backlight shows translucency)
- Clear spirits (glowing edge effect)
- Transparent cocktails (light transmission)
- Ice close-up (internal texture visible)

---

### Side Lighting

**When to Use**
- Highlighting bottle texture
- Building three-dimensionality
- Condensation droplet shots
- Label detail rendering

**Key Points**
- Light source from side at 45–90°
- Creates light-and-shadow contrast
- Highlights surface texture and depth
- Shadows add drama

**Setup**
```
Key light: side at 60°
Intensity: medium to high
Hardness: medium hard (softbox can be used)
Reflector: opposite side to fill shadows
```

**Prompt Keywords**
- side lighting, directional light
- chiaroscuro, high contrast
- textured surface, dimensional
- dramatic shadows

**Best Products**
- Whiskey bottle (barrel wood texture)
- Beer can (condensation droplets)
- Wine bottle (label detail)
- Frosted bottle (surface texture)

---

### Soft Diffused Light

**When to Use**
- Lifestyle scenes
- Atmospheric still life
- Cocktail art photography
- Soft gradient backgrounds

**Key Points**
- Large-area soft light source
- Soft shadow transitions
- Even overall illumination
- Warm, comfortable color temperature

**Setup**
```
Light source: large softbox or skylight
Position: top or side-top at 45°
Diffusion: multiple layers of diffusion material
Fill light: reflector for reflection fill
```

**Prompt Keywords**
- soft diffused lighting, gentle illumination
- even lighting, no harsh shadows
- natural light quality
- subtle gradients

**Best Products**
- Coffee drinks (soft atmosphere)
- Wine dining scene
- Home beverage photography
- Pink cocktails

---

### Dramatic Lighting

**When to Use**
- Premium spirits
- Black background products
- Luxury brand imagery
- Male-oriented products

**Key Points**
- Strong light-shadow contrast
- Localized focal illumination
- Large areas of deep shadow
- Spotlight effect

**Setup**
```
Key light: spotlight or small hard light source
Angle: side or side-top
Background: pure black or deep color
Fill light: minimal or none
```

**Prompt Keywords**
- dramatic lighting, spotlight effect
- high contrast, chiaroscuro
- dark moody atmosphere
- cinematic lighting

**Best Products**
- Whiskey, brandy
- Premium wine
- Dark bottle products
- Cigar and spirits scene

---

### Ambient Lighting

**When to Use**
- Bar atmosphere photography
- Bokeh background effect
- Lifestyle candid feel
- Dusk outdoor scenes

**Key Points**
- Simulate real environmental light
- Bokeh effect creates atmosphere
- Warm tones dominant
- Light feels natural and soft

**Setup**
```
Key light: multiple small warm light sources
Position: scattered in background area
Effect: blurred into bokeh spots
Foreground light: soft fill light on product
```

**Prompt Keywords**
- ambient lighting, atmospheric glow
- bokeh lights, out of focus background
- warm ambiance, cozy atmosphere
- environmental lighting

**Best Products**
- Bar scene whiskey
- Restaurant wine
- Nighttime cocktails
- Fireside spirits

## Special Lighting Effects

### Bokeh

**How to Create**
- Use wide aperture (f/1.4 – f/2.8)
- Point light sources distributed in background
- Increase distance between subject and background
- Long focal length enhances effect

**Prompt Implementation**
```
shallow depth of field, bokeh lights in background, 
circular light spots, blurred background, 
out of focus bokeh effect
```

**Classic Applications**
- Bar background bottle rack lights → circular warm bokeh
- Night city lights → multicolored light spots
- Christmas string lights → colored bokeh

---

### Highlight Control

**Key Points**
- Bottle highlights not blown out
- Liquid surface has sparkle points
- Ice cube highlights are crystalline
- Glass edge shows bright line

**Control Methods**
- Use polarizer to reduce reflections
- Adjust light angle to avoid direct hits
- Diffusion to lower brightness
- Post-process to lower highlight areas

**Prompt Keywords**
- controlled highlights, specular highlights
- glowing highlights without overexposure
- bright accents, sparkle

---

### Shadow Management

**Soft Shadows**
- Large light source or softbox
- Reflector to fill shadows
- Multi-directional fill light
- Best for lifestyle scenes

**Hard Shadows**
- Small hard light source
- No or minimal fill light
- Adds drama
- Best for concept photography

**Prompt Keywords**
- soft shadows, gentle shadow transition (soft)
- sharp shadows, defined shadows (hard)
- minimal shadows, shadowless (no shadow)

## Lighting Combination Plans

### Plan A: Classic Three-Point Lighting

**Best for**: Still life atmosphere, product close-up

**Setup**
```
Key Light: side-front at 45°, medium intensity
Fill Light: opposite side reflector, soft fill
Rim Light: side-backlight, outlining edges
```

**Effect**: Strong depth, rich layers, commercial standard

---

### Plan B: Pure Backlight

**Best for**: Dynamic splash, liquid transparency

**Setup**
```
Key light: direct backlight, high intensity
Front fill: faint fill light (optional)
Background: pure black light-absorbing material
```

**Effect**: Dramatic, glowing liquid, high contrast

---

### Plan C: Ambient + Fill Light

**Best for**: Lifestyle narrative, lifestyle feel

**Setup**
```
Ambient: natural light or simulated environmental light
Main fill: soft directional light to enhance subject
Reflector: micro-adjust local lighting
```

**Effect**: Natural and real, rich atmosphere, approachable

---

### Plan D: Top Light Even Illumination

**Best for**: Macro close-up, top-down scenes

**Setup**
```
Key light: large softbox top illumination
Fill light: surrounding reflectors
Shadow elimination: even diffusion
```

**Effect**: Shadowless, detail sharp, accurate color

---

### Plan E: Single-Side Hard Light

**Best for**: Creative concept, minimalist style

**Setup**
```
Single light source: side hard light
No fill light: preserve deep shadows
Background: solid color or gradient
```

**Effect**: Minimal, modern, strong artistic quality

## Common Problem Solutions

### Problem 1: Messy Glass Reflections

**Solutions**
- Use polarizer to filter
- Adjust light source angle
- Black flags to absorb stray light
- Control reflective objects in shooting environment

**Prompt Strategy**
Add: clean glass surface, minimal reflections, controlled lighting

---

### Problem 2: Dull Liquid Color

**Solutions**
- Use backlight or transmitted light
- Increase background brightness
- Place white reflector behind liquid
- Increase light source intensity

**Prompt Strategy**
Add: vibrant liquid color, backlit showing color depth, luminous

---

### Problem 3: Ice Not Transparent Enough

**Solutions**
- Use pure water to make ice
- Backlight or transmitted light to illuminate
- Control exposure to highlight ice
- Macro to show detail

**Prompt Strategy**
Add: crystal clear ice, pristine ice cubes, backlit ice showing clarity

---

### Problem 4: Background Bokeh Not Obvious

**Solutions**
- Increase aperture (f/2.8 or wider)
- Increase distance between subject and background
- Use longer focal length
- Pointify background light sources

**Prompt Strategy**
Add: shallow depth of field, strong bokeh effect, highly blurred background

---

### Problem 5: Overall Light Too Flat

**Solutions**
- Reduce fill light to increase contrast
- Use hard light source
- Single-side lighting
- Increase shadow area

**Prompt Strategy**
Add: dramatic lighting, high contrast, defined shadows, directional light
