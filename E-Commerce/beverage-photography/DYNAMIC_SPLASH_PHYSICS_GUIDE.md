# Dynamic Splash Physical Plausibility Guide

## Problem Statement

**User feedback**:
> "When pouring in the Dynamic Splash style, if no one is holding the bottle, the product is floating in mid-air — isn't that unrealistic? Maybe show only the partial pour (no hand visible), but if the full bottle is shown pouring, there should be a hand holding it."

**Core issue**:
- **Physical implausibility**: A full bottle floating mid-pour defies gravity and basic physics
- **Reduced commercial credibility**: Unrealistic imagery lowers advertising believability
- **Need a flexible solution**: Achieve dynamic effect while maintaining physical plausibility

## Error Case Analysis

### ❌ Error 1: Full Bottle Floating Mid-Pour

```
Prompt: "Full [Product] bottle tilted in air, pouring liquid into glass, 
         dynamic splash, floating bottle"
```

**Problem**:
- Full bottle clearly visible
- Bottle at tilted angle (45°–60°)
- Liquid pouring out
- **No support whatsoever (no hand, no stand)**
- Bottle "floating" in air

**Why it's wrong**:
- Violates basic physics (gravity)
- Viewers subconsciously sense it's "fake" and "unrealistic"
- Like a levitation trick, but this isn't a magic ad
- Reduces product credibility and premium feel

### ❌ Error 2: Bottle Pouring Without a Hand

```
Prompt: "[Product] bottle pouring into glass, liquid flowing, 
         bottle at 45 degree angle, dynamic pour"
```

**Problem**:
- Full bottle visible, tilted pouring
- No explicit "hand holding" instruction
- AI may generate a floating bottle

**Result**: 50% chance of physically implausible image

## Correct Approaches

### ✅ Option A: Full Bottle + Hand Holding (Recommended for Lifestyle Scenes)

**Best for**:
- Emphasizing human-product interaction
- Lifestyle, authentic, storytelling scenes
- Showcasing usage experience

**Prompt Template**:
```
Dynamic commercial photography: [Product] being poured into glass with elegant hand.

POURING ACTION:
- Hand gracefully holding [Product] bottle/can at [neck/body]
- Bottle tilted at natural angle (30-45 degrees)
- Liquid flowing from bottle into glass, creating gentle splash
- Hand visible: [choose style below]

HAND STYLE OPTIONS:
Option 1 - Elegant female hand:
  "Elegant female hand with manicured nails, delicate grip on bottle neck,
   refined gesture, soft skin tone"

Option 2 - Strong masculine hand:
  "Masculine hand with natural skin tone, firm grip on bottle body,
   confident pouring motion"

Option 3 - Partially visible hand:
  "Hand partially visible at bottle base, fingers wrapped around bottle,
   focus on pouring action rather than hand detail"

FROZEN MOMENT:
- High-speed freeze (1/8000s shutter), liquid droplets suspended mid-air
- Splash in glass captured at peak moment
- Ice cubes and liquid in dynamic motion

PHYSICAL REALISM:
- Hand position anatomically correct
- Natural grip (not awkward or stiff)
- Bottle weight supported (believable hold)

AESTHETIC: [selected aesthetic style]

AVOID: floating bottle, no support, physically impossible poses
```

**Advantages**:
- ✅ Physically plausible (hand provides support)
- ✅ Realistic and credible (real-use scenario)
- ✅ Emotional connection (human involvement)
- ✅ Premium feel (elegant hand gesture)

**Notes**:
- Hand must be natural and elegant, not stiff
- Grip position must be reasonable (bottle neck is most common)
- Hand should not dominate (can be partially blurred or partially visible)
- Nail color and skin tone should suit brand tone

---

### ✅ Option B: Partial Pour (Neck Only) — No Hand Required (Recommended for Product Focus)

**Best for**:
- Emphasizing the product and liquid texture
- Avoiding hand interference with product focus
- Purer product photography style

**Prompt Template**:
```
Dynamic commercial photography: Close-up of [Product] pouring moment.

COMPOSITION:
- Close framing on bottle neck/opening
- Liquid flowing out from opening, creating stream
- Bottle body extends beyond frame top (implied: held outside frame)
- Glass positioned below, receiving liquid

POURING DETAILS:
- Liquid stream clearly visible, flowing smoothly
- Splash in glass, frozen moment (1/8000s)
- Droplets suspended in air, ice cubes in motion
- Focus on liquid texture, color, and transparency

FRAMING TECHNIQUE:
- Tight crop: only show bottle neck/top 1/3 of bottle
- Bottom of frame: glass with splash
- Implication: hand holds bottle above frame (not visible, but implied)
- This creates "someone is pouring" feeling without showing hand

PHYSICAL REALISM:
- Pouring angle natural (30-45 degrees implied by liquid trajectory)
- Liquid flow physics correct (gravity, arc, velocity)
- No floating bottle visible (cropping solves this)

AESTHETIC: [selected aesthetic style]

RESULT: Dynamic pour without hand distraction, physically plausible through framing
```

**Advantages**:
- ✅ Physically plausible (hand outside frame, implied to exist)
- ✅ Product focus (no hand interference)
- ✅ Dynamic effect (pour and splash)
- ✅ Clean composition (product as visual center)

**Key Technique**:
- Use **cropping** to resolve the physics problem
- Hand outside the frame (implied but not visible)
- Liquid trajectory implies the pour angle
- Viewers automatically "imagine" the hand outside frame

---

### ✅ Option C: Splash Close-up + Static Product (Recommended for Visual Impact)

**Best for**:
- Maximizing visual impact of liquid splash
- Presenting product as the "static hero"
- Avoiding pour action, focusing on the splash moment

**Prompt Template**:
```
High-speed commercial photography: Dynamic splash with [Product] hero shot.

SPLASH FOCUS:
- Liquid splash frozen mid-air (ice water/beverage/alcohol)
- Multiple droplets suspended, creating dramatic spray
- Ice cubes flying, water droplets caught at peak moment
- Splash height: 10-30cm above glass

PRODUCT PLACEMENT:
- [Product] bottle/can placed statically on surface
- Position: beside glass, slightly behind, or in background
- Product perfectly still (no motion, no pouring)
- Label clearly visible, product in spotlight

FROZEN MOMENT:
- Ultra high-speed capture (1/8000s to 1/12000s feel)
- Every droplet sharp and defined
- Ice cube mid-rotation, water beads spherical
- Splash at peak dynamic moment

PHYSICAL SETUP:
- Splash appears to be from: liquid poured into glass (completed action), 
  OR ice cube dropped into liquid (impact splash)
- [Product] is NOT the source of splash (just positioned in scene)
- Splash origin: glass itself (liquid already in glass, ice drop impact)

AESTHETIC: [selected aesthetic style]

RESULT: Maximum visual impact, product as static hero, no floating bottle issue
```

**Advantages**:
- ✅ Completely avoids floating bottle physics issue
- ✅ Maximizes splash visual impact
- ✅ Product presented perfectly as static hero
- ✅ Works with all aesthetic styles

**Splash Source Options**:
- Ice cube dropped into glass (ice impact creates splash)
- Liquid poured into glass (pour completed, capturing splash aftermath)
- Cocktail glass collision (cocktail scene)

## Selection Guide

### By Aesthetic Style

**Soft Natural Style** 🌸
- Recommended: Option A (elegant hand pour)
- Reason: Emphasizes warmth and human touch; hand adds lifestyle feel
- Hand style: Elegant female hand, delicate and gentle

**High-Saturation Architectural Style** 🎨
- Recommended: Option C (splash close-up)
- Reason: Emphasizes visual impact; product contrasts strongly with architectural background
- Splash effect: Explosive force, vivid color contrast

**Dramatic Atmospheric Style** 🎭
- Recommended: Option C (splash close-up) or Option B (partial pour)
- Reason: Creates mystery; suspended splash in light beams
- Lighting focus: Drama of liquid in light and shadow

**Minimalist Commercial Style** ⚪
- Recommended: Option C (splash close-up, simplest composition)
- Reason: Avoids complex hand elements, keeps it minimal
- Composition: product + splash, solid color background

### By Product Type

**Premium Spirits** (whiskey, brandy)
- Recommended: Option A (elegant hand pour)
- Hand style: Mature masculine or refined feminine
- Pour gesture: Elegant, deliberate, ceremonial

**Beer / Sparkling Water**
- Recommended: Option C (splash close-up)
- Splash effect: Explosive, ice-cold, rich bubbles
- Product placement: Can perfectly static

**Cocktails**
- Recommended: Option A (bartender hand)
- Hand style: Professional bartender technique
- Scene: Bar counter, cocktail environment

**Juice / Soft Drinks**
- Recommended: Option B (partial pour)
- Focus: Juice color, fresh flowing feel
- Splash: Gentle, refreshing

- ❌ "levitating bottle in pour action"

## Quality Checklist

After generating a Dynamic Splash image, check:

- [ ] **Physical plausibility**: Does the pour action follow physical common sense?
- [ ] **Option identification**: Which option is this — A / B / C?
- [ ] **If Option A**:
  - [ ] Is the hand visible?
  - [ ] Does the hand gesture look natural?
  - [ ] Is the grip position reasonable?
  - [ ] Is the hand-to-product proportion balanced?
- [ ] **If Option B**:
  - [ ] Is only the bottle neck/opening shown?
  - [ ] Does the liquid trajectory imply the pour angle?
  - [ ] Does the crop look natural (not awkward)?
- [ ] **If Option C**:
  - [ ] Is the product static (no pour action)?
  - [ ] Is the splash dynamic enough?
  - [ ] Is the splash source plausible (ice impact / liquid collision)?
- [ ] **Issues to avoid**:
  - [ ] No full bottle floating mid-pour
  - [ ] No physically implausible image
  - [ ] No "levitating" bottle

## Summary

**Core Principles**:
1. **Full bottle pouring → hand must be present**
2. **Partial pour (neck only) → hand not required**
3. **Splash close-up → product is static**

**Selection Strategy**:
- Choose option based on aesthetic style
- Choose hand style based on product positioning
- Balance dynamic feel and focus based on scene needs

**Prevention Mechanism**:
- Explicitly state the chosen option in the Prompt
- Emphasize physical plausibility keywords
- Use quality checklist to verify
