# Illustration Styles Reference

Human-readable companion to `prompt-library.json`. Use this to understand each style's visual language, video reinforcement strategy, and when to recommend it.

---

## Art Nouveau Botanical (`art_nouveau`)

**Visual DNA**: Ornate decorative borders, flowing organic curves, botanical precision with decorative flair. Think Alphonse Mucha posters meets botanical illustration. Flat color fills with visible ink linework. Gilt gold accents and ornamental frames.

**Video reinforcement**: "hand-drawn ink outlines visible, flat color fills, no photographic textures in background, ornamental botanical borders" -- this keeps the Art Nouveau linework and flat fills visible throughout the animated clip, preventing Seedance from smoothing them into photorealism.

**Best for**: Products with botanical/natural ingredients, French heritage brands, floral fragrances, skincare with plant-based stories. The ornate framing naturally elevates the product.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on an illustrated vanity surrounded by Art Nouveau ornamental details -- hand-drawn bottles, decorative mirror, botanical prints on the wall
- Bold: Product in its ingredient landscape -- Art Nouveau vineyard, botanical garden, herb field with ornamental borders
- Visionary: Product as the centerpiece of an ornate Art Nouveau poster that comes to life -- the borders bloom and grow

**World examples**: Lush botanical gardens, vineyard landscapes, rose gardens, herb fields, ornamental greenhouse interiors, Art Nouveau archways with climbing vines.

**Animation**: Flowers bloom and unfurl, vines grow and curl, butterflies flutter between illustrated flowers, gentle camera pans across ornate botanical scenes.

**Color**: Rich jewel tones with gold accents -- deep emerald greens, ruby reds, warm amber, gilded borders.

## Impressionist Painterly (`impressionist`)

**Visual DNA**: Visible brushstrokes, soft color blending, Monet/Renoir-inspired light. The world looks like an oil painting with impasto texture. No hard outlines -- forms are defined by color contrast and light.

**Video reinforcement**: "visible brushstrokes throughout, oil painting texture, no digital smoothing, painterly color blending" -- this keeps the brushstroke texture visible during animation, preventing Seedance from smoothing into photorealistic rendering.

**Best for**: Luxury fragrances, premium skincare, products with French or European heritage. Works especially well for products with transparent/glass materials that catch painted light.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on a windowsill painted in soft morning light -- Impressionist brushstrokes on the curtains, the view outside, the table surface
- Bold: Product in its origin landscape as a painting -- Provencal lavender fields, Monet water lily ponds, sunlit meadows
- Visionary: Product enters a famous painting that comes to life -- museum gallery scene, the painting dissolves into moving Impressionist world

**World examples**: Provencal lavender fields, Monet water lily ponds, sunlit meadows, dappled forest light, stone bridges, coastal cliffs at golden hour, Parisian interiors.

**Animation**: Gentle light shifts across the painted scene, water reflections ripple softly, painted petals float on breeze, slow camera drift.

**Color**: Soft pastels -- lavender, sage green, warm sunlight yellow, chalky blue sky, blush pink.

## Line-Art Botanical (`line_art`)

**Visual DNA**: Clean ink outlines with flat or lightly textured color fills. Precise botanical detail with an illustrative quality. Influenced by manga environmental art and Studio Ghibli backgrounds. Varying line weight creates depth.

**Video reinforcement**: "clean ink outlines visible, flat color fills, hand-drawn line quality, no photographic shadows in background" -- this preserves the clean linework during animation, which is the style's most distinctive feature.

**Best for**: Products targeting younger demographics, clean/organic brands, Japanese beauty products, any product where a detailed but approachable illustration style fits.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on an illustrated desk or shelf -- Ghibli-style room with hand-drawn books, plants, a cup of tea, window showing an illustrated garden
- Bold: Product surrounded by its ingredients drawn with botanical precision -- detailed herb illustrations, vine drawings, flower studies
- Visionary: Product in a Studio Ghibli-style fantasy landscape -- a tiny illustrated village built around the product, characters interacting with it

**World examples**: Detailed garden scenes, old stone walls with wildflowers, village rooftops, forest clearings, herb markets, coastal trails, balcony gardens.

**Animation**: Parallax layer movement (foreground faster than background), wind rustling through illustrated leaves, butterflies flying, slow panoramic pans.

**Color**: Muted earth tones with selective vivid accents -- sage green, dusty pink, warm cream, terracotta.

## Comic Panel / Storybook (`comic`)

**Visual DNA**: Bold outlines, flat color blocks, sequential art panel layout. Can range from pop-art energy to soft storybook pastels. The panel/frame structure creates natural sequential narrative.

**Video reinforcement**: "bold outlines visible, flat color blocks, no smooth gradients in background, storybook illustration quality" -- this keeps the flat fills and bold outlines from being softened into photorealistic gradients during animation.

**Best for**: Products with playful brand identities, limited editions, holiday/seasonal campaigns, Gen-Z targeting. The comic format is inherently shareable.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product in a comic-panel morning routine -- sequential panels showing wake up, reach for product, use it, smile -- each panel is an illustrated snapshot
- Bold: Product origin story told as a comic strip -- ingredient harvest, creation process, the product emerging at the end
- Visionary: Product as a magical object in a storybook -- illustrated characters discover it, it transforms their world panel by panel

**World examples**: Stylized cityscapes, fantasy landscapes, whimsical interiors, storybook cottages, enchanted forests, pop-art backgrounds.

**Animation**: Camera zooms into a comic panel that comes to life, illustrated elements pop in one by one, page-turn transitions, camera tracking across panels.

**Color**: Two modes -- (1) Bold saturated primaries for energetic/pop products, (2) Soft storybook pastels for gentle/luxury products.

## Watercolor (`watercolor`)

**Visual DNA**: Soft wet edges where pigment bleeds into textured paper. Translucent washes layered over each other. Visible paper grain beneath the paint. Color pooling in organic shapes. No hard outlines -- forms are defined by the edge of the wash. Feels handmade, gentle, organic.

**Video reinforcement**: "watercolor wash texture visible, soft bleeding edges, paper grain texture, no hard digital edges in background" -- prevents Seedance from sharpening the deliberately soft edges into crisp digital renders.

**Best for**: Gentle/organic skincare, tea brands, artisanal products, candle/home fragrance, any product positioning around softness, calm, or handmade quality.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on an illustrated table -- watercolor washes form a soft morning scene, painted teacup, painted window light bleeding across paper-textured surfaces
- Bold: Product's ingredients as watercolor botanical studies -- pigment blooming into flower forms, color washes suggesting ingredient origins
- Visionary: Product triggers a watercolor bloom -- dry paper world that floods with spreading color washes radiating from the product

**World examples**: Soft kitchen mornings, garden terraces, botanical studies on cream paper, gentle landscapes with bleeding skies, still life arrangements with translucent overlapping washes.

**Animation**: Color washes spread and bloom across paper, pigment pools form slowly, soft fade-ins as if painted in real time, gentle camera drift across a painted surface.

**Color**: Soft and translucent -- pale sage, dusty rose, diluted amber, sky blue washes, warm cream (paper showing through).

## Art Deco (`art_deco`)

**Visual DNA**: Geometric precision and glamour. Bold symmetrical patterns, sunburst motifs, chevron and fan shapes. Metallic gold and silver accents. Angular architectural forms. Influenced by Erté fashion illustrations and Tamara de Lempicka paintings. The aesthetic of 1920s luxury -- grand hotels, ocean liners, jazz-age evenings.

**Video reinforcement**: "geometric patterns visible, sharp angular forms, metallic accents, no organic softness in background architecture" -- maintains the hard geometric precision that defines Art Deco, preventing Seedance from softening angles into organic curves.

**Best for**: Luxury fragrance, fashion accessories, jewelry, watches, champagne/spirits, any product with architectural or glamour positioning. Especially strong for products with gold or metallic packaging.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on an illustrated Art Deco vanity -- geometric mirror frame, angular perfume tray, sunburst wall pattern, black marble surface
- Bold: Product's origin as a Deco narrative -- the factory/atelier as a geometric palace, ingredients transformed through angular machinery
- Visionary: Product as the centerpiece of an Art Deco architectural monument -- geometric towers radiating from the product, sunburst patterns expanding outward

**World examples**: Grand hotel lobbies, geometric archways, sunburst ceiling patterns, angular cityscapes at night, mirrored surfaces, geometric garden layouts, ballroom interiors.

**Animation**: Geometric patterns reveal layer by layer, light catches and slides across metallic surfaces, symmetrical elements unfold from center, slow architectural pans.

**Color**: Black, gold, silver, deep emerald, ivory, burgundy. High contrast with metallic highlights.

## Ukiyo-e — Japanese Woodblock (`ukiyoe`)

**Visual DNA**: Flat color planes without shading, bold flowing outlines, Hokusai and Hiroshige nature motifs. Waves, clouds, and mountains rendered as stylized graphic forms. Limited color palette (5-7 colors max). Distinctive woodblock print texture. Compositions often use strong diagonals and layered depth planes.

**Video reinforcement**: "flat color planes visible, bold outlines, woodblock print texture, no photographic gradients in background" -- maintains the flat, graphic quality of woodblock prints, preventing Seedance from adding 3D shading or realistic gradients.

**Best for**: Japanese beauty brands, zen/wellness products, tea, ceramics, any product with Japanese heritage or nature-forward positioning. Also works for products wanting a distinctive, graphic-yet-serene aesthetic.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on an illustrated tatami surface -- sliding screen door showing a Hokusai-style landscape beyond, flat-color tea implements nearby
- Bold: Product's ingredients as a woodblock nature scene -- stylized waves of ingredient essence, mountain forms of botanical origins, flat-color flower fields
- Visionary: Product as a monumental form in a Hokusai landscape -- great wave curling around it, Mount Fuji-scale illustrated world, the product as a still point in dynamic nature

**World examples**: Great wave compositions, mountain landscapes with layered cloud banks, cherry blossom branches, koi ponds, torii gate pathways, tea house interiors, moonlit ocean scenes.

**Animation**: Waves flow in stylized patterns, clouds drift as flat graphic forms, cherry blossoms fall in geometric paths, slow lateral scroll (emulating scroll painting viewing).

**Color**: Limited palette -- indigo blue, vermillion red, pale green, warm ochre, ivory, black outlines. Restrained and graphic.

## Chinese Ink Wash — Shanshui (`chinese_ink`)

**Visual DNA**: Ink gradients ranging from dense black to pale grey washes. Calligraphic brushstroke quality -- thick to thin, wet to dry. Mountain-water (shanshui) landscape tradition. Intentional negative space where the rice paper shows through. Misty atmosphere. The aesthetic of scholarly simplicity and contemplative depth.

**Video reinforcement**: "ink wash gradients visible, calligraphic brush quality, rice paper texture, no solid fills or hard outlines in background" -- maintains the gradient ink quality, preventing Seedance from converting washes into solid fills or adding outlines that don't exist in ink painting.

**Best for**: Chinese heritage brands, tea (especially Chinese tea), traditional medicine, calligraphy-related products, any product with Chinese cultural roots or zen/contemplative positioning. Also works for products emphasizing simplicity and negative space.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on an illustrated scholar's desk -- ink-wash bamboo, a painted teacup, calligraphic scroll, misty mountain visible through an ink-wash window
- Bold: Product's ingredients as ink-wash landscapes -- tea leaves as misty mountains, herbs as calligraphic brushstrokes, water as flowing ink
- Visionary: Product at the center of a shanshui landscape that materializes from ink -- brushstrokes painting themselves into mountains, mist flowing from the product outward, a world emerging from negative space

**World examples**: Misty mountain landscapes, bamboo groves, waterfall scenes, scholar's study interiors, lotus ponds, pine trees on cliffs, river valleys in fog.

**Animation**: Ink spreads and pools on paper, mist drifts through mountain passes, water flows as ink gradients, slow vertical scroll (emulating hanging scroll viewing).

**Color**: Monochromatic ink gradients (black to grey to white) with optional accent of one color -- vermillion red, jade green, or amber gold. The restraint is the point.

## Pop Art (`pop_art`)

**Visual DNA**: Bold primary colors at full saturation. Ben-Day dots as texture and shading. Thick black outlines. High contrast with minimal mid-tones. Warhol's serial repetition and Lichtenstein's comic-derived graphic style. The aesthetic of mass culture elevated to art -- loud, confident, unapologetic.

**Video reinforcement**: "Ben-Day dot patterns visible, bold flat primary colors, thick black outlines, no subtle gradients in background" -- maintains the graphic, high-contrast look, preventing Seedance from softening into natural color gradients.

**Best for**: Gen-Z brands, streetwear, playful/bold beauty, limited editions, energy drinks, sneakers, any product with a bold, youthful, or counter-cultural identity. Strong for social media -- the graphic impact reads well at small sizes.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product in a Pop Art kitchen/bathroom -- flat-color appliances, Ben-Day dot wallpaper, bold graphic shadows, Lichtenstein-style speech bubble ("WOW!")
- Bold: Product's ingredients as Pop Art icons -- oversized graphic flowers, bold-color ingredient close-ups, serial repetition of key elements
- Visionary: Product multiplied Warhol-style across a color grid -- each iteration in a different bold palette, the grid animating and shifting, the product as cultural icon

**World examples**: Graphic cityscapes, Ben-Day dot skies, bold-color interiors, oversized consumer objects, comic-panel environments, neon-lit scenes.

**Animation**: Elements pop in with graphic impact, color blocks flash and shift, quick punchy zooms, fast pan across repeated elements.

**Color**: Full-saturation primaries -- red, yellow, blue, plus black and white. No pastels, no muted tones. Occasionally hot pink or electric green for accent.

## Paper Cut / Collage (`paper_cut`)

**Visual DNA**: Layered paper textures with visible cut edges casting slight shadows between layers. Torn and cut paper shapes forming the composition. Matisse's late cut-outs as primary influence -- organic forms from simple cuts. Collage elements can include textured papers, fabric textures, and patterned sheets. Depth created by parallax between layers rather than perspective.

**Video reinforcement**: "paper texture visible, layered cut edges with shadows between layers, no smooth digital surfaces in background" -- maintains the tactile, handmade quality, preventing Seedance from smoothing paper textures into clean digital surfaces.

**Best for**: Craft/artisanal brands, modern editorial products, packaging-conscious brands, sustainable/eco products, stationery, any product that benefits from a handmade/craft aesthetic. Also strong for fashion and editorial contexts.

**Direction pairings**:
- Safe: Product on a paper-cut table scene -- layered paper surfaces, cut-paper plant forms, torn-edge window showing a collaged landscape
- Bold: Product's ingredients as botanical paper cuts -- layered paper leaves, petals as cut shapes with visible paper texture, ingredient journey as collage narrative
- Visionary: Product surrounded by an expanding paper-cut world -- layers pulling apart to reveal depth, paper forms unfolding into a three-dimensional collage diorama

**World examples**: Layered paper landscapes, cut-paper botanical gardens, collaged interiors with mixed paper textures, paper-fold architectural forms, torn-edge nature scenes.

**Animation**: Paper layers slide in parallax (foreground faster than background), paper pieces fold and unfold to reveal new elements, shadows shift between layers as camera moves, gentle drift revealing depth between paper planes.

**Color**: Depends on paper selection -- can range from muted craft-paper tones (kraft brown, cream, sage) to vivid colored-paper palette (Matisse's bold blue, red, yellow, green). Shadow between layers is always visible.

## Choosing a Style

| Product Signal | Recommended Styles |
|---|---|
| Botanical/natural ingredients | Art Nouveau, Line-Art, Watercolor |
| French/European luxury heritage | Impressionist, Art Nouveau, Art Deco |
| Japanese brand or zen positioning | Ukiyo-e, Line-Art |
| Chinese brand or heritage | Chinese Ink Wash |
| Playful/youthful brand | Comic, Pop Art |
| Premium fragrance | Impressionist, Art Deco |
| Clean/organic positioning | Line-Art, Watercolor |
| Holiday/seasonal campaign | Comic, Art Nouveau |
| Heritage/artisanal brand | Impressionist, Chinese Ink Wash |
| Luxury fashion/accessories | Art Deco |
| Craft/sustainable brand | Paper Cut, Watercolor |
| Bold/streetwear/Gen-Z | Pop Art, Comic |
| Gold/metallic packaging | Art Deco |
| Minimalist/modern brand | Line-Art, Paper Cut |

The style is independent of the direction (Safe/Bold/Visionary/Cinematic). Any style can pair with any direction. The Cinematic tier uses the same visual style but adds an illustrated character — the character is rendered in the chosen style, matching the world. See SKILL.md for character design and consistency guidance.
