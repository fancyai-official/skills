# FancyAI Fashion & Editorial Skills

Welcome to the **FancyAI Fashion & Editorial Skills** set. This collection is purpose-built to solve one of the most persistent challenges in modern fashion creative production: **translating high-level creative vision into production-ready assets** — from campaign direction to editorial storytelling, design development, and street style documentation.

## Why We Built This

Created by the [FancyAI Team](https://fancyai.com), this skill set is a direct response to real-world friction we observed while working alongside fashion directors, creative agencies, and in-house teams at global brands.

Across **1000+ brand collaborations** — from sportswear (Adidas, Skechers, HOKA) to luxury (LVMH, Burberry) and contemporary fashion (Stuart Weitzman) — we learned that the single biggest missing ingredient in AI-generated fashion content is not technical accuracy. It's **sense**.

**We built these skills to encode the taste of master designers into AI.** These workflows are not theoretical — they are summarized from years of experience serving prestigious brands. We've distilled what works, what doesn't, and what separates ordinary content from truly fashionable assets — then translated that knowledge into structured, repeatable skills.

These skills learn from the masters, then adapt to your brand's DNA. People can use our skills to create **more premium, taste-driven fashion assets** — campaign images, editorial storyboards, garment designs, and street style photography that feel intentional, sophisticated, and authentically fashionable.

## What This Set Solves

This folder contains fashion-specific skills that teach AI how to think like **professional fashion creative experts**. Each skill provides:

| Skill | What It Does |
| :--- | :--- |
| `fashion-campaign` | A full AI Creative Crew (Creative Director, Photographer, Stylist, Hair & Makeup, Casting Director, Set Designer) that analyzes your apparel inputs and produces a complete campaign — 3 treatment pitches, then 6–9 campaign images. |
| `fashion-editorial` | Generates six fixed 3:4 editorial storyboard images from multi-angle white-background product photos, with styling references, pose vocabulary, and per-frame quality control. |
| `fashion-design` | Lets you design with a legendary fashion icon (Chanel, McQueen, Armani, Valentino, Dior, or Saint Laurent) — outputting front, side, and back on-model imagery. |
| `street-style-photography` | A street style photographer in the spirit of *The Impression* and *Vogue* — turning garment or outfit images into candid fashion week street style photos. |

## How These Skills Are Structured

Each skill in this folder is self-contained in its own folder with a `SKILL.md` file. While each skill serves a different fashion creative function, they share a common structure:

- **Role-based expertise** — Each skill embodies a specific fashion creative role (campaign director, editorial photographer, designer, street style documentarian)
- **Structured workflows** — Clear step-by-step processes from input (garment photos, design briefs, product assets) to output
- **Taste encoding** — Each skill contains the visual vocabulary, styling references, and creative decision-making frameworks of fashion professionals
- **Quality control** — Built-in mechanisms for refinement, iteration, and targeted regeneration
- **Scalable output** — From single frames to multi-image campaigns, output scales with input volume

Whether you're producing a 6-frame editorial storyboard, a 9-image campaign, or a 3-angle garment design, the underlying structure is the same: **professional fashion expertise, encoded for AI.**

## Get Started

1. Choose the skill that matches your fashion creative need:
   - **Campaign production** → `fashion-campaign`
   - **Editorial storytelling** → `fashion-editorial`
   - **Design development** → `fashion-design`
   - **Street style documentation** → `street-style-photography`

2. Copy the skill folder into your AI assistant's skills directory (e.g., `~/.claude/skills/`)

3. In your AI conversation, reference the skill by name and describe your creative brief:
   - *"Use the fashion-campaign skill to develop a mood board for a spring/summer collection launch."*
   - *"Use the fashion-editorial skill to plan an avant-garde editorial story for a print magazine."*
   - *"Use the fashion-design skill to help sketch a capsule collection focused on sustainable fabrics."*

4. Specify whether you need **photo** (still), **video** (motion), or both

5. Iterate using the provided templates — refine concepts, adjust shot lists, or evolve design directions
