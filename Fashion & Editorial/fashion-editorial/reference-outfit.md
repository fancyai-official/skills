# Styling: general vocabulary and global social UGC trends (combined reference)

This file merges **general outfit vocabulary** with **global social UGC / OOTD-style trend tags** (Instagram / Pinterest / TikTok–style abstractions—**not** tied to any one region): the first half is **category × bottoms × shoes/socks × palette × scene** completion logic; the second half is public trend tags and formula cheat sheets. Both must align with **brand, triple-choice, and hero SKU**—**do not swap the hero garment**. Defaults match the main Skill: **no bag, no earrings, no necklace** by default; no competitor logos; use neutral material/silhouette wording.

## Apparel styling · general vocabulary

### 1. Styling decision order (internal checklist)

1. **Lock mood axis**: street / sport / minimal / outdoor / sweet / commute, etc.—aligned with **step 1 brand** and **step 3 triple-choice**; no clashing axes across the set.  
2. **Lock palette**: from hero piece extract **main color + neutral base** (black, white, gray, denim blue, khaki, oatmeal, etc.); at most **one** small accent color so nothing steals focus.  
3. **Lock bottoms**: pick one of wide-top slim-bottom / simple-top wide-bottom / tonal continuity—avoid top and bottom fighting each other.  
4. **Lock shoes/socks**: plausible for scene (no dress shoes on sand, etc.) and **same pair for the whole set** unless frame or client says otherwise.  
5. **Lock layers**: inner neck, hem visibility; jacket open/closed; **no pointless extra pieces**.

### 2. Bottoms by hero category (abstract)

| Hero mood | Common bottom directions (pick one) | English phrases (pick) |
|----------|--------------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Sport hoodie / jacket | Joggers, cargos, wide denim, sporty mini | `joggers or cargo pants`, `wide-leg denim`, `sporty mini skirt` |
| Knit / soft top | Fluid wide-leg, straight denim, midi skirt | `fluid wide-leg pants`, `straight denim`, `midi skirt soft drape` |
| Shirt / light top | Tailored trousers, chino, denim, minimal shorts | `tailored trousers`, `chino pants`, `clean denim`, `minimal shorts` |
| Structured jacket / down | Straight or slight-flare denim, utility, clean knit pants | `straight jeans`, `utility pants`, `clean knit pants` |
| Dress (whole-piece SKU) | Shoes/socks finish; minimal layering that fights the dress | `cohesive shoes`, `sheer or opaque tights if season fits` |

### 3. Shoes/socks vs mood

| Scene / mood | Shoe abstraction | Socks |
|--------------|------------------|-------|
| Street / sport | Retro runners, German trainers, chunky sneakers, clean court shoes | White/gray crew or sport socks, or tonal with pants |
| Korean / fresh | German trainers, canvas, simple loafers | Crew or no-show, clean, no busy pattern |
| Minimal / commute | Loafers, Mary Janes, low ankle boots | No-show or tone-match with bottoms |
| Outdoor / tech | Hikers, trail runners, simple sandals (summer) | Tech crew or tone-match with shoes |
| Vacation / beach | Sandals, espadrille leisure, clean slip-on leisure | No socks or nude liner |

Internal: `matching footwear to outfit story`, `same shoe style family as top`, `cohesive socks or no-show socks`.

### 4. Palette formulas (short)

- **Tonal carry**: top main + bottoms one step lighter/darker + shoes echo main.  
- **Neutral base + one color**: black/white/gray/denim base + **one** accent from the hero on shoe or inner edge.  
- **Denim bridge**: denim bottoms pair with most casual tops; match **wash depth** to overall contrast.  
- **Avoid**: two loud clashing saturates, or neon stacks that fight brand mood.

Examples: `tonal outfit`, `neutral base one accent color`, `denim anchor cohesive wash`, `unified warm palette` / `cool monochrome`.

### 5. Layers and inners (no competitor names)

- **Tee / tank**: neck shape works with outer (crew, V, racer); hem may peek for layer.  
- **Shirt**: as inner—show collar tip or hem; as outer—open placket, tuck or untuck **consistent across set**.  
- **Knit over shoulders / waist tie**: editorial touch, **do not cover chest logo**; use on some frames only, not every frame.

Phrases: `layered necklines`, `hem peeking under jacket`, `open shirt over base tee`, `clean layering without clutter`.

### 6. Outfit vs scene

| Scene type | Styling note |
|------------|--------------|
| Beach / boardwalk | Leisure footwear, light bottoms or shorts; avoid heavy dress boots |
| Café / dessert exterior | Urban casual or Korean-clean; shoes not overly rugged tech unless brand is |
| Stadium / outdoor | Sport bottoms or denim; shoes support “motion” |
| Terrace / urban skyline | Slightly dressy (trousers, loafers) still matches hero mood |

### 7. Consistency across 6 frames

- **Same inner, bottoms, shoes**: frames 2–6 do not randomly change shoes/pants; if a frame is upper-only, **same outfit still holds** off-frame.  
- **Accessories**: hat/sunglasses/headphones share **one color or material story**; not required every frame.  
- **With pose sheet**: sitting, full body, lean must keep **inseam, hem, shoes** visible and consistent—see [reference-pose-vocabulary.md](reference-pose-vocabulary.md) **§6 Shot scale and pose pairing**.

### 8. Pre-prompt checklist (richness)

When user/client **has not** specified a full outfit, before writing internal prompt confirm (**not** stacking random pieces—**information complete**):

- [ ] **Mood axis** matches step 1 brand and step 3 triple-choice; no axis clash.  
- [ ] **Main + neutral base** from hero; if accent, **at most one** small area.  
- [ ] **Bottoms** chosen from §2 for category with English phrase.  
- [ ] **Shoes + socks** from §3 match scene and mood; **same shoes** whole set unless frame/client exception.  
- [ ] **At least one layer** written (§5: neck, hem, jacket open, waist, etc.).  
- [ ] If pulling from **“Social UGC & trend styling”** below, only **structural abstraction**, **no** hero swap.  
- [ ] Default **no bag, no earrings, no necklace**; no competitor logos.

### 9. Taboos (match main Skill)

Do not swap hero; do not default bag or ear/neck jewelry; signage follows [reference-scenes.md](reference-scenes.md). **Trend and formula detail** lives in **“Social UGC & trend styling”** below; this section is the **underlying outfit skeleton**.

## Social UGC & trend styling: trend tags and structure

For **internal** abstraction in `editorial-fashion-six`; must align with **brand, triple-choice, hero SKU**—not a substitute for the hero. Trends are from public industry reads—**not mandatory line by line**. **General category / bottoms / shoes / palette skeleton** is in **“Apparel styling · general vocabulary”** above.

### Common trend tags (keywords)

| Tag (EN label) | Abstract outfit structure (one line) |
|----------------|----------------------------------------|
| Off-duty ease | Sport-casual + commute-friendly, relaxed shoes and bottoms |
| Soft power / soft-fit | Silk / fine knit / linen feel, soft lines, restrained palette |
| Airy layering | Light layers, breathable, short-over-long or cardigan, avoid bulk |
| Staycation | Moderate skin and vacation mood, still scene- and brand-true |
| Rebel preppy | Plaid / pleats + asymmetric or raw hem, young not grungy |
| Sportique | Sport cut + street or soft detail; shoes/socks match top mood |
| Blokette | Zip track jacket × contrast bottom (pleated midi, tulle, lace, plaid tiered, etc.), hard + soft tension |
| Tweed chic | Cropped tweed / gold buttons, black/white/cream/camel, high shorts or pleated mini |
| Preppy | Pleated mini, loafers + visible socks, shirt/blazer layers, bookish |

### Formula cheat sheet (public trend abstraction)

Common **completion formulas** for internal **phrases** in prompt; must match **hero SKU, brand, triple-choice**—**no style swap**. Reference photos often show big bags, earrings, necklaces—when that **conflicts with this Skill**, **Skill wins** (default no bag, no earrings/necklace); use **sunglasses, coffee cup, adjusting hem** for story instead.

#### A. Track jacket + skirt (Blokette / sporty-sweet)

- **Structure**: zip track, windbreaker, sleeve stripe × **soft or contrast bottom** (A-line long, tiered tulle/lace, plaid pleated midi, denim mini, etc.).
- **Proportion**: wide or sporty top × long/soft bottom for tension.
- **Shoes/socks (abstract)**: white socks + loafers or sneaker family, boots; sock height pushes street/preppy.
- **English examples** (pick, don’t stack): `zip track jacket with feminine midi skirt`, `sporty top soft long skirt contrast`, `white crew socks with loafers or sneakers`, `balanced sporty-feminine silhouette`.

#### B. Tweed / quiet luxury (tweed + gold buttons)

- **Structure**: short **tweed/bouclé** jacket, crew or blazer, **gold buttons**; inner white tee, shirt, or turtleneck; bottoms **high shorts, mini pleats, wide tailored trousers**.
- **Palette**: black, white, cream, camel, gold accent, restrained.
- **Shoes/socks**: ballet flat, Mary Jane, loafer; optional **sheer black tights** or **white ankle socks** consistent with scene (whole set).

#### C. Urban editorial layering

- **Structure**: stripe shirt × strapless mini over; or **tweed jacket + white tee + denim**; knit over shoulder for layer.
- **Examples**: `layered shirt and structured mini dress`, `tweed jacket white tee denim`, `editorial urban layering`.

#### D. Preppy / rebel preppy

- **Structure**: blazer / badge jacket, pleated mini, loafers + **visible socks**; or plaid, raw edges, “young not dirty.”
- **Examples**: `preppy pleated mini loafers`, `academic layering neat silhouette`.

#### E. One jacket across scenes (reuse idea)

Same statement jacket with **wide trousers + flat** (relaxed) or **shorts + slingback/loafer** (seasonal leg); for **unified set** “bottom mood” choice without extra clutter.

### Abstraction dimensions (short words for prompt)

- **Silhouette**: tight-top wide-bottom, wide leg, mini + tall socks, high waist lengthens legs, etc.
- **Palette**: tonal, neutral base + one accent, echo hero main color.
- **Layers**: inner hem out, shirt at waist, light stack, neck stack.
- **Shoes/socks**: match scene + hero mood (sport / loafer / Mary Jane / sandal, etc.), unified set unless frame needs otherwise.

### Taboos (match main Skill)

Do not swap hero; do not stack brand-clashing styles; when abstracting from trends **default no bag** (shoulder/crossbody/tote, etc.), **default no earrings or necklace** (including choker), same as main Skill; **no competitor logos or full brand names**—use `generic track jacket`, `striped sleeves`, etc.; signage follows [reference-scenes.md](reference-scenes.md).
